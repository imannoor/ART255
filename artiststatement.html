Artist Statement

In my art, I strive to be authentic, colorful, and abstract. I choose to be authentic because I make art for myself. I hope that other people enjoy it; however, I do not keep that as an end goal in mind. Instead, as cheesy as it sounds, I do what makes me happy. I want my art to be colorful because I want it to reflect my life. I paint things I love, and I paint with colours I love. One of those is the colour pink. In all shades. This is evident in a lot of my work with pink being an overwhelming theme between some of my works. Strangely enough, I was not even aware of this connection, as some of the works that are mainly pink date years away from my most recent “pink” work. I make my work abstract because I do not take my art too seriously, and I enjoy making art that may not have a clear meaning and direction.

In my art, I love working with all medians. Water colour, oil pastels, digital art, acrylic paints and ceramics. I use any median that interests me as I enjoy trying new things. Another art piece I love creating are collages. I love making them into mixed medias by including fabrics, and other objects like gems and stones to give it more depth and texture.

Although art is not my major, nor my main focus in my life I enjoy it a lot. I even use it within my major of linguistics where I try to use art to facilitate English second language learning. That is why I love art so much. It is integral part of so many different part areas of our lives and I hope that this book inspires you to make art, even if you do not think you are an Artist.
